A Guide To EDM
A random page letting you know what is what in Electronic Dance Music
  • Dubstep

    Dubstep basically has a hard drop. Some people (such as myself) dislike too hard of a Dubstep because it ends up losing the melody.

  • Complextro

    A paused and 'bumpy' drop, with various different tunes in it, one after the other. Sometimes using different instruments to create even more musical chaos. An indeed complex feeling, it sounds complicated overall. And it is.

    Example (drop):

  • Electro

    Electro's main characteristic is the synth drop. Most have a good melody along with the synth.

    Example (drop):

  • Swing

    A sub-genre, usually of Electro (Electro Swing), it brings back the feeling of antique and fancy things, sounds as if it was being played through a gramophone. Piano, trumpets, violins etc are common.

  • House

    House is usually well defined by the repetition of a 4/4 compass. Stuff only changes at the end of each cycle.

    Example (drop):

  • Big Room

    It literally sounds as if you were in a big room, with some echo and stuff. Has dry beats, and attempts a majestic drop. Tons of repetition. Hated by a lot of people for being "too easy to produce".

  • Trance

    It's like Electro, but with some wubs and dubs in it. It has hard beats like Hardcore, mixed with the synth.

    Trap's main 'thing' is the deep bass on drops, and many times the milliseconds of silence before a drop. Also, a characteristic snare and clap that usually are only heard on trap tunes. [Note: Trap + Chillstep = Best combination ever]
